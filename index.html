Understanding Financial Metrics

Learn what each metric means and how to interpret the results for better investment decisions.

Investment Modes

Lumpsum Investment

One-time investment of the entire amount at the beginning.

Best for: Large amount available, immediate market exposure.

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

Regular monthly investments of a fixed amount.

Best for: Disciplined investing, rupee cost averaging.

Performance Metrics

Absolute Returns

Simple percentage gain/loss without time factor.

Use: Quick comparison of total gains.

CAGR (Lumpsum)

Compound Annual Growth Rate for lumpsum.

Use: Compare across time periods.

XIRR (SIP)

Internal Rate of Return for multiple cash flows.

Use: True annualized return for SIP.

Risk Metrics

Maximum Drawdown

Largest peak-to-trough decline.

<10%: Low • 10-20%: Moderate • >20%: High
Average DD Duration

Typical recovery time from drawdowns.

<30d: Quick • 30-90d: Moderate • >90d: Slow
Maximum DD Duration

Longest period in drawdown.

<6m: OK • 6-12m: Hard • >1y: Patience
